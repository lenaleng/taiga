Why Forest Preschool

The Benefits of Nature-Based Learning

Nature immersion profoundly influences child development. Forest preschools encourage physical activity, imagination, and a deeper connection to the environment. Children develop resilience and problem-solving skills in the dynamic outdoor classroom.

Learn more about how our approach fosters curiosity, emotional well-being, and social cooperation among children in our San Francisco programs.

Research & Evidence

A Potential Natural Treatment for Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder

This nationwide study reveals that "green" or natural settings significantly reduce ADHD symptoms in children across diverse subpopulations. The findings suggest that common activities conducted in relatively natural outdoor environments are a widely accessible, inexpensive, and side-effect-free way to improve attention and focus.

Residential Green Space in Childhood is Associated with Lower Risk of Psychiatric Disorders

This comprehensive study of nearly one million individuals found a strong prospective association between exposure to green space during childhood and a significantly lower risk of developing psychiatric disorders later in life. Children growing up with the highest levels of green space had up to a 55% lower risk of mental health issues from adolescence into adulthood.
