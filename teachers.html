Our Teachers

Meet the wonderful educators who guide our children every day.

Elena Lengardt

Founder & Lead Teacher

Elena Lengardt is the founder of the Russian-speaking Forest Preschool in Golden Gate Park, San Francisco.

Originally from Kazan, Russia, Elena holds a degree in economics and previously worked at PwC, where she developed a strong sense of responsibility, organization, and professionalism. She later pursued a career as an artist, developing a deep appreciation for creativity, observation, and curiosity about the world.

After moving to the United States four years ago and becoming a mother, Elena’s perspective shifted toward early childhood development. While searching for the right environment for her own daughter, Karina, she realized how important it was to create a place where young children could spend more time outdoors, exploring nature, moving freely, and learning through real experiences.

Inspired by this vision, Elena created a program she would confidently choose for her own child — a nature-based, child-centered forest preschool where children can grow curious, resilient, and connected to the natural world.

Elena is currently studying Early Childhood Education at City College of San Francisco and has completed 18 credits toward her certificate. She believes deeply in lifelong learning and continues to expand her knowledge of child development and education.

Her background in the arts also shapes the program’s approach: children are encouraged to observe nature closely, express themselves creatively, and explore the world with imagination and curiosity.

As both a mother and educator, Elena’s goal is simple: to create the kind of place where she would be happy to bring her own child every day.

You will most often find Elena in Golden Gate Park with the children — exploring trails, observing birds, collecting leaves, and discovering the small wonders of nature together.
